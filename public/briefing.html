During Event — Your Role
  • You are assigned to a specific table — own it for the whole event
  • When speaker is talking — you are silent. No side conversations. Full attention on the speaker. This is respect, and it models the right behaviour for participants.
  • When piano music plays = exercise time — get up, walk behind your group, look over shoulders, coach quietly and individually
  • Stay on high alert listening to the speaker — know what's being asked so you can help your table execute it
  • Raise your hand (don't speak) if your group needs more time — Kingsley will see it. You are the ground gauge for pace.
Good vs Poor
✓ Good
  • Silent and focused when speaker is talking
  • Up and moving the moment piano plays
  • Every person at your table makes progress
  • You catch someone stuck before they raise their hand
  • Hand goes up (silently) when your table isn't ready
✗ Poor
  • Standing at the back joking and chatting with other facis — disrespectful to speaker and kills the room energy
  • Talking when the speaker is talking
  • Sitting while participants struggle
  • Missing the piano cue and staying seated
  • Not listening to speaker so you don't know what your table should do
Post-Event
  • Join debrief call with Kingsley and Huda
  • Share what your table struggled with most
  • Note common blockers to improve next session

Common Objections — How to Handle
"It's too expensive / I need to think about it"
→ "I understand. What I can tell you is — the earlier tier closes once it fills up and price goes up. What specifically are you unsure about? Let's work through it now." Then listen. After addressing — extend your hand: "Let's lock it in at this price today."
"I'm not tech-savvy enough"
→ "You just spent the whole morning building something you didn't know how to build. That's the proof. This programme is designed exactly for people who feel that way."
"I need to check with my spouse / partner"
→ "Totally fair. Can you call them now? I'll give you 5 minutes and I'll be right here. The tier won't last long." Stay nearby — don't disappear.
"I don't have time to implement"
→ "The whole point is to save you time. What you built today took 3 hours. Imagine doing that in your business every week. The time investment is the shortcut."
"Can I join next time instead?"
→ "The next run will be at a higher price. The best time to lock in is today. We have limited seats and this tier won't be available again." Then extend your hand and walk them to the QR code.
The Close — Shake Hands, Sign Up

Once objections are handled, don't ask — lead. Extend your hand, make eye contact, and say:

"Let's do this together. Scan here, put in your details, and we'll get you set up right now."

Then stand next to them while they complete checkout. Don't walk away until payment is confirmed.

Before Every Interview — Brief the Participant
"Before we start — when I ask you a question, begin your answer by repeating part of it back. If I ask 'What were you struggling with before today?' start with 'Before today, I was struggling with…' This makes it much easier to edit."
